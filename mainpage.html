Abstract Expressionism

What is it? A Brief History

Abstract expressionism is an American Art movement originating in New York City in the 1940s. It originated as an exploration of raw emotions and feelings. Artists in this movement were affected by the horror of World-War II and rejected painting human subjects in favor of an abstract style.

Key Artists

  • Jackson Pollock
  • Lee Krasner
  • Barnett Newman
  • Mark Rothco
  • Willem de Kooning

Influences

Many artists in the movement were influenced by famous psychologists such as Carl Jung and his theory of the subconcious mind. Other major contributions include the art movements of Surrealism and Cubism.

Legacy

Abstract Expressionism died out in the 60's but remain highly influential. It affected movements such as Color Field painting, Lyrical Abstraction, Fluxus, Pop Art, Minimalism, Postminimalism, Neo-expressionism, and more. Movements were also made in direct responses to it include Hard-edge painting and Pop Art.
