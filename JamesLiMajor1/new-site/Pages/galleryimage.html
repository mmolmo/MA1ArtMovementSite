Abstract expressionism is an American Art movement originating in New York City in the 1940s. Artists in this movement were affected by the horror of World War II and rejected painting human subjects in favor of an abstract style. In response to the tragedy, these artists created the movement as an exploration of pouring raw emotions and feelings onto the canvas.
